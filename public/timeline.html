Bean's Journey

1978

The Dawn of a Mamba

Kobe Bryant was born on August 23, 1978, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, to former NBA player Joe Bryant.

1996

Drafted to the NBA

Kobe was selected 13th overall by the Charlotte Hornets in the 1996 NBA Draft but was immediately traded to the Los Angeles Lakers. He was just 17 years old at the time, making him the youngest player ever drafted into the NBA. Kobe quickly became the youngest player to play in an NBA game, and his potential was immediately clear. His arrival in LA began a new era for the Lakers.

1996

First NBA Game

Kobe made his NBA debut with the Lakers.

1998

First All-Star Game

At just 19 years old, Kobe became the youngest NBA All-Star in history when he was selected for the 1998 All-Star Game. In addition to his performance, this moment marked the beginning of his long-standing rivalry and relationship with other NBA superstars. Kobe's selection proved his immense talent and his importance to the Lakers, setting the stage for his rise as one of the league’s top players.

2000

First NBA Championship

In the year 2000, Kobe won his first NBA championship alongside Shaquille O'Neal, defeating the Indiana Pacers in six games. This championship was the first of three consecutive titles (2000-2002), which began the Lakers' early 2000s dynasty. Kobe was pivotal in the Finals, showcasing his talent on both ends of the floor and cementing his legacy as one of the NBA's most valuable players.

2000-2002

Three-Peat

The Lakers, with Kobe and Shaq leading the charge, went on to win three consecutive championships from 2000 to 2002. This three-peat solidified the Lakers as a dynasty and Kobe as a future Hall of Famer. His performances, including clutch shots in the Finals, proved that he was one of the best players in the league and a force to be reckoned with. During this time, he also began to develop into the leader the Lakers would later rely on for future titles.

2003

All-Star Game vs. Michael Jordan

In 2003, Kobe Bryant faced Michael Jordan for the first time in an All-Star Game, a moment that many fans will never forget. Kobe, who grew up idolizing Jordan, now found himself on the same court as the man he tried to emulate. In a thrilling duel, Kobe’s performance was a testament to how far he had come as a player, as he showcased his scoring ability, footwork, and basketball IQ against his idol.

2006

81-Point Game

In one of the greatest individual performances in NBA history, Kobe scored 81 points against the Toronto Raptors on January 22, 2006, the second-highest point total in a single game in NBA history (only behind Wilt Chamberlain's 100-point game). Kobe was unstoppable that night, demonstrating his offensive prowess and ability to take over a game. This performance remains one of the most iconic in the history of professional basketball.

2008

MVP Season

In 2008, Kobe Bryant won his first and only NBA Most Valuable Player (MVP) award. He had an outstanding regular season, leading the Lakers to the top of the Western Conference while averaging 28.3 points per game. His dominance on both ends of the floor earned him recognition as the league’s best player that season, and he came close to winning his first title in several years before falling short to the Boston Celtics in the Finals.

2010

Fifth Championship

Kobe secured his fifth and final NBA championship in 2010, defeating the Boston Celtics in a grueling seven-game series. This victory not only cemented his legacy as one of the greatest players of all time, but it also gave him his fifth ring, placing him in rarefied air alongside NBA legends like Michael Jordan and Magic Johnson. Kobe was named Finals MVP, leading the Lakers with his scoring, defense, and leadership throughout the series.

2016

Last All-Star Game

Played his final All-Star Game, receiving a standing ovation from fans and players alike.

2016

Farewell Game

On April 13, 2016, Kobe Bryant played his final game in the NBA, scoring an incredible 60 points in a victory against the Utah Jazz. The game was an emotional farewell to a legendary career, and Kobe’s performance showcased his enduring greatness. The entire NBA world came together to honor the “Black Mamba” on this historic night. He retired as the all-time leading scorer for the Lakers and left an indelible mark on the game of basketball.

2018

Oscar Win

In 2018, Kobe Bryant won an Academy Award for Best Animated Short Film for his work on "Dear Basketball." This win made him one of the few athletes to ever receive an Oscar, showcasing his creativity and storytelling abilities beyond the basketball court. The film, which was based on a poem he wrote about his love for the game of basketball, was celebrated by both the sports and entertainment industries.

2020

Kobe's Passing

On January 26, 2020, Kobe Bryant tragically passed away in a helicopter crash, along with his daughter Gianna and seven others. His death sent shockwaves across the globe, with fans, athletes, and celebrities honoring his legacy and impact on the game and beyond.

2020

Hall of Fame Inductee

Kobe Bryant was posthumously inducted into the Basketball Hall of Fame in 2020. This recognition came after his tragic passing in January 2020, and it solidified his place as one of the greatest players in the history of the game. His Hall of Fame induction was a tribute to his incredible career and legacy both on and off the court.

2025

Kobe's Impact Continues

In 2025, Kobe's legacy continues to influence basketball, sports culture, and beyond. His foundation, charitable work, and his impact on young athletes are still growing. His story is immortalized in museums, documentaries, and his spirit is alive in every player that aspires to greatness.

Father. Son. Mamba.
